Overview of "Vatican II: The Battle for Meaning"

The book "Vatican II: The Battle for Meaning" by Massimo Faggioli explores the ongoing debate surrounding the interpretation and reception of the Second Vatican Council (1962-1965). Faggioli argues that the Council was an epoch-making event whose significance is still being contested within the Catholic Church.

The book is structured to guide the reader through the history of these debates, the various theological schools of thought that emerged, and the key controversies that define the post-conciliar era. Rather than presenting a single interpretation, Faggioli illuminates the complex landscape of competing narratives about what Vatican II meant and continues to mean for the Catholic Church.

This study examines how the Council has been understood from the time of its conclusion to the early 21st century, covering periods of initial reception, enforcement under different pontificates, and the scholarly "historicization" that has shaped contemporary understanding.

Chapter Summaries

Chapter 1: A Short History of the Debate

Traces the history of how Vatican II has been understood from the Council itself to the early 21st century, covering initial reception, enforcement periods, and scholarly historicization.

Chapter 2: Questioning the Legitimacy

Examines radical reactions to the Council, from traditionalist groups who reject its legitimacy to those who felt reforms didn't go far enough.

Chapter 3: Vatican II Beyond Rome

Explores the global reception of the Council, its ecumenical impact, and the rise of new theological movements like Liberation Theology.

Chapter 4: Augustinians and Thomists

Analyzes the deep theological divisions between Neo-Augustinian and Progressive Neo-Thomist schools of thought that shaped Council interpretation.

Chapter 5: The Clash of Narratives

Focuses on intense historical research after 1985, examining conflicting narratives about what "really happened" at the Council.

Chapter 6: Macro-Issues of the Debate

Synthesizes core issues at the heart of the debate, exploring whether Vatican II was an end or beginning of renewal.
